Her Story


Rani Lakshmibai was born as Manikarnika (Manu) in Varanasi. From a young age, she was brave and loved horse riding, sword fighting, and learning skills usually taught to warriors.
After her marriage to the King of Jhansi, she became the queen. But her life changed when her husband died, and the British tried to take over Jhansi using unfair policies.
She refused to surrender and chose to fight for her kingdom. During the Revolt of 1857, she led her army with great courage and fought against the British forces.
In 1858, she died on the battlefield, but her bravery made her a legend in Indian history.

She lived with courage and died as a legend.
